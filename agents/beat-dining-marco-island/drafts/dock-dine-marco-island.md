# Dock & Dine: 6 Waterfront Restaurants from Marco Island

The best waterfront restaurants around Marco Island aren't on the highway — they're on the water. Here's where to dock, eat, and drink when you're cruising Southwest Florida's coast.

## 1. Stan's Idle Hour (Goodland)

This is the one. Legendary Old Florida at its finest. Sunday music on the deck, cold beer, zero pretense. Dock right out front and walk straight to the bar. If you only do one dock-and-dine from Marco Island, make it Stan's.

**The vibe:** Ramshackle waterfront institution. Live music, locals, and boaters who know.

## 2. Little Bar (Goodland)

Right next door to Stan's. Better food, craft beer selection, waterfront deck with views. Hit both in one trip — Stan's for the scene, Little Bar for dinner.

**The move:** Dock at Stan's, walk between both.

## 3. CJ's on the Bay

Marco Island waterfront at the Esplanade. Upscale dining with a tiki bar side. Beautiful sunset views over the Gulf. Dock space available.

**The draw:** Refined waterfront dining without leaving Marco.

## 4. Snook Inn

Classic Marco Island waterfront restaurant off the Marco River. Chickee hut bar, live music, dock access. Reliable fresh seafood and a scene that's been here for decades.

**What to order:** Whatever's fresh. The fish doesn't lie.

## 5. The Dock at Crayton Cove (Naples Run)

Run north to Naples Bay. Worth the trip for the best waterfront dining in the area. Prime dockage, upscale atmosphere, views across the harbor.

**The distance:** 20 minutes by boat from Marco. Make it a destination.

## 6. Capri Fish House

On the water at Isle of Capri, between Marco and Naples. Fresh catches, old Florida atmosphere, no crowds. The spot locals keep quiet about.

**Why it works:** Off the beaten path. Great food. Easy docking.
