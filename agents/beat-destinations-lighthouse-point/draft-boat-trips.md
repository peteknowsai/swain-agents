# 5 Best Boat Trips from Lighthouse Point

Your home port sits at one of the best spots on Florida's east coast — here's where to point the bow. Port32 Lighthouse Point is right at Hillsboro Inlet, one of SE Florida's most important inlets. The Gulf Stream is only 15-20 miles offshore — closer than almost anywhere else on the coast. Reef fishing starts less than a mile offshore once you clear the inlet. That proximity to blue water is what makes this spot special.

Most of these runs are under 30 minutes. That's the Lighthouse Point advantage.

## 1. Hillsboro Inlet Anchorage

**Distance:** Right at your doorstep
**Run time:** 5 minutes

Start with the easiest trip — anchor near the iconic Hillsboro Lighthouse and watch boats navigate the inlet. This is your front yard. The lighthouse has been guiding vessels since the mid-1800s and serves as a critical navigation landmark. Lake Santa Barbara provides excellent protected anchorage just inside the inlet, perfect for testing systems or enjoying sunset without burning fuel.

**What to know:** The lighthouse offers monthly tours for members. Some boaters anchor ocean-side near floating markers, though regulations have tightened in recent years. Still, it's a spectacular spot to sit and watch the parade of boats heading offshore or coming back loaded with fish.

## 2. Deerfield Beach Pier

**Distance:** 3 nautical miles south
**Run time:** 15-20 minutes

Short run down the coast to one of the area's best fishing piers. The 976-foot Deerfield Beach International Fishing Pier draws anglers chasing King Mackerel and other species you'd normally find in deeper water. From your boat, you get beach views and can fish the structure near the pilings.

**What to know:** The pier is open 6 AM - 11 PM daily. If you're running low on bait or tackle, the pier concession stocks fresh and frozen bait. Great spot for a quick morning fish before heading offshore, or an afternoon cruise with the family. The beach scene here is more laid-back than Fort Lauderdale.

## 3. Boca Raton Sandbars

**Distance:** 8 nautical miles south via ICW
**Run time:** 25-30 minutes

Lake Boca's shallow sandy bottom makes it the premier weekend raft-up spot in South Florida. The sandbar at the north end of the lake gets busy by midday on fair-weather weekends — dozens of boats, hot bodies, and plenty of socializing. This is where Boca Bash happens every April (last Sunday), drawing massive crowds.

**What to know:** Navigate cautiously — Lake Boca is very shallow. Maintain at least 3-3.5 feet of depth and follow channel markers along the outer rim if you're not anchoring immediately. Visit on high tide for easier navigation and that signature turquoise water. Anchor on the north end in deeper water where your boat is safer and you're away from the knee-deep sandbar crowds.

## 4. Lake Boca (Protected Anchorage)

**Distance:** 8 nautical miles south via ICW
**Run time:** 25-30 minutes

Same destination, different vibe. Lake Boca on a weekday is a peaceful protected anchorage — perfect for swimming, paddleboarding, or just floating. The water is calm, clear, and beautiful. During the week, you'll have space to spread out and actually relax.

**What to know:** The center sandbar protrudes toward the north end, so use channel markers to navigate around the perimeter. Come at high tide for the best water clarity and easiest navigation. This spot has everything you want in an anchorage except on weekends. If you're bringing the family or want a quiet day, hit it Monday through Thursday.

## 5. Peanut Island

**Distance:** 12 nautical miles south (ICW or offshore)
**Run time:** 30-35 minutes

The crown jewel of Palm Beach County. Peanut Island is a world-famous sandbar and snorkel lagoon near the Palm Beach Inlet. The sandbar to the north of the island transforms into a massive boat party on peak holiday weekends — beach chairs, grills, umbrellas partially submerged in shallow water. The snorkeling area to the southeast has artificial reefs loaded with sea turtles, manatees, tropical fish, and corals.

**What to know:** Snorkeling is best at high tide. The island also has the JFK bunker tour — a fascinating Cold War relic. On busy weekends, expect a sea of boats converging on the sandbar. If you want a more chill experience, anchor away from the main sandbar or visit on a weekday. You can run down the ICW or make it a short offshore cruise depending on conditions.

---

**Pro tip from a local captain:** All five of these destinations are within 35 minutes of Port32 Lighthouse Point. That's the beauty of this location — you're close to everything. Protected anchorages, party sandbars, fishing piers, and snorkel spots. And when you're ready for the real adventure, the reef is a mile away and the Gulf Stream is 15 miles out. You're sitting at one of the best home ports in Florida.
