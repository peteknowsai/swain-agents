# Naples by Boat: 8 Destinations You Can't Reach by Car

Some of Southwest Florida's best places exist only for those who own a boat. From pristine barrier islands to hidden fishing villages, these are the destinations that make Naples one of the world's premier boating communities.

## 1. Keewaydin Island — The Crown Jewel

No roads. No bridges. Only by boat.

Keewaydin Island stretches nearly eight miles along the Gulf, offering some of the most pristine and uncrowded beaches in Southwest Florida. This barrier island is 80% undeveloped wilderness, making it a true escape from the developed coastline.

**What to Know:**
- Anchor on the Gulf side in calm conditions
- Excellent shelling, especially after storms
- Pet-friendly beach (leash required to protect nesting sea turtles)
- Popular weekend anchorage with floating food boats
- Important loggerhead sea turtle nesting site

**Access:** Boat only. Launch from Rose Marina on Marco Island or Naples-area marinas. Most boaters anchor in shallow water off the beach.

## 2. Ten Thousand Islands — Old Florida Wilderness

South of Marco Island lies an endless maze of mangrove islands that define raw, undeveloped Florida. This is true backcountry — shallow channels, hidden beaches, and wildlife everywhere.

**What to Expect:**
- Navigate narrow mangrove channels with strong tidal currents
- World-class fishing for snook, redfish, and tarpon
- Dolphins, manatees, river otters, osprey, and bald eagles
- Remote shelling on islands like Kice Island
- Launch points in Goodland and Port of the Islands

**Navigation Note:** Study the tides before heading out. Plan your trip for an outgoing tide to avoid fighting current in the narrow channels.

## 3. Gordon Pass Sandbar — The Social Scene

Right at the mouth of Naples Bay, Gordon Pass offers easy access to a lively sandbar scene. This is where locals gather on weekends.

**Why It's Popular:**
- Anchor on the calm bay side
- Swim in clear, shallow water
- Watch boats navigate the pass
- Excellent sunset viewing
- Short run from Naples marinas

**Caution:** Gordon Pass experiences strong tidal currents. Following 2021 dredging, the pass now maintains minimum 8-foot depths, but pay attention to tide changes when anchoring nearby.

## 4. Marco Island — Dock, Dine, and Explore

A short run south from Naples via the Intracoastal Waterway or open Gulf, Marco Island offers full marina services and walkable access to restaurants and shops.

**Top Marinas & Restaurants:**
- **The Esplanade Marina** — 77 floating concrete slips on Smokehouse Bay, adjacent to CJ's on the Bay for upscale waterfront dining
- **Marina at Factory Bay** — Located in historic Olde Marco with easy access to shops and dining
- **Dolphin Tiki Bar & Grill** — Classic open-air tiki bar with boat-up access
- **Mango's Dockside Bistro** — Dog-friendly with marina views

**Distance from Naples:** Approximately 15-20 nautical miles depending on route.

## 5. Delnor-Wiggins Pass State Park — North to Paradise

Head north from Naples to this pristine state park beach. One of the area's most beautiful and less-crowded stretches of coastline.

**Features:**
- Anchor just off the white sand beach
- Excellent shelling and snorkeling
- Boat ramp access into Water Turkey Bay ($5 launch fee plus park entrance)
- Can navigate to Cocohatchee River for freshwater fishing

**Important:** No vessels may anchor within 100 feet of the designated fishing area at the pass. The park reopened in February 2025 after hurricane repairs.

## 6. Rookery Bay — National Estuarine Research Reserve

Explore 110,000 acres of pristine mangrove forest and protected waters stretching from Gordon Pass to the western Everglades. This is Southwest Florida's ecological treasure.

**Activities:**
- Paddle or idle through protected mangrove channels
- Spot dolphins, manatees, and osprey
- Outstanding birdwatching
- Guided eco-tours available seasonally (November-May)

**Launch Point:** Isles of Capri Paddlecraft Park or rent kayaks with delivery to south Naples/Marco Island. The Rookery Bay Environmental Learning Center ($10 adults, open Tue-Sat 9am-4pm) offers educational exhibits and tour information.

## 7. Goodland — Funky Old Florida

This working fishing village on the northern edge of the Ten Thousand Islands remains authentically Old Florida. Dock your boat and step into a different era.

**The Experience:**
- **Stan's Idle Hour** — Legendary waterfront bar and restaurant, accessible by boat or car
- Private docks available across from Stan's ($8 launch at Goodland Boating Park)
- Parking limited (13 spaces), so arriving by boat is ideal
- Sunday Funday atmosphere with live music

**Location:** Via Marco Island, just south of the main island on the Ten Thousand Islands edge.

## 8. Everglades Backcountry — Wilderness Fishing

From Chokoloskee Bay into the Everglades, this is world-class tarpon country and total wilderness. Serious boaters and anglers only.

**What Makes It Special:**
- Peak tarpon season: May, June, July along beaches and passes
- Backcountry tarpon (10-60 pounds) in creeks and rivers after August
- True wilderness experience
- Launch from Gulf Coast Visitor Center (between Everglades City and Chokoloskee) or Flamingo

**Requirements:**
- Free online boating course certificate required (show at check-in)
- Backcountry camping permits: $11/night for two people, plus $30 park pass
- Advance reservations required for chickee, beach, and ground camping sites

**Navigation:** Shallow water, narrow channels, and remote conditions. Know your boat's draft and bring navigation charts.
