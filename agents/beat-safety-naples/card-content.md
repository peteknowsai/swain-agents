## Historic Freeze — Snow Flurries in Naples

Something extremely rare just happened: **snow flurries** fell across Southwest Florida early Sunday morning, from Fort Myers to Naples. This is the coldest air in 15 years, with temperatures near or below freezing region-wide.

Cape Coral is monitoring its canal system for potential fish kills—the first major event in over a decade.

## How Cold Affects Your Boat

Even in Southwest Florida, this freeze demands attention:

**Engine Systems:**
- Drain water from engine block and manifolds
- Check raw water intake for blockages
- Run antifreeze through cooling systems if leaving inactive

**Battery Protection:**
- Cold drains batteries faster
- Fully charged batteries resist freezing
- Consider disconnecting and storing indoors
- Test charge before starting

**Pre-Start Checklist:**
- Inspect fuel lines and connections
- Check bilge for standing water
- Verify battery charge

> Southern Florida generates the highest freeze-related insurance claims because boaters underestimate occasional cold snaps.

---

## Fish Behavior in Cold Water

**Snook:**
Go dormant under docks and in canals. Extremely cold-sensitive—risk of cold kill if low temps persist.

**Redfish:**
Slow down significantly. Still on shallow flats but feeding drops off. Vulnerable to cold stunning.

**Sheepshead:**
Unlike other species, sheepshead stay active and feed aggressively. Look for them near warm water discharges.

**Cold-stunned fish** become lethargic, can't evade predators, and their metabolism slows dangerously.

## If You See Dead Fish

**Report immediately to FWC:**
📞 **1-800-636-0511**
🌐 myfwc.com

**Include:**
- Location (GPS or address)
- Date/time noticed
- Number of fish and species

**Critical:**
- ❌ DO NOT handle dead or dying fish
- ❌ DO NOT eat affected fish

FWC uses these reports for critical research—about 2,000 people report annually.

---

## When Conditions Improve

**Be patient.** Cold-stunned fish are extremely vulnerable.

- Practice **catch-and-release** until waters warm
- Give fish populations time to recover
- Snook and redfish need protection after cold events
- Water temps can fluctuate rapidly—recovery takes days
