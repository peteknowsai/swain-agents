## What's Within Reach

**Hillsboro Inlet** — 10 minutes north. Smaller, less traffic, quick access to the reef.

**Port Everglades Inlet** — 10 minutes south. Deep, wide, commercial-grade channel. The fastest route to deep water.

**The Reef Line** — 20 minutes east. Three tiers of reef structure running parallel to the coast. Snapper, grouper, kingfish, and everything that hunts them.

**100+ Miles of Intracoastal** — North to Jupiter, south to Miami. Sandbars, waterfront restaurants, state parks, and endless cruising.

## Local Knowledge: Pro Tips

**Timing the Inlet** — Port Everglades sees heavy commercial traffic. Avoid early morning (cruise ship departures) and late afternoon (container ships inbound). Weekends are cleaner. Hillsboro is your backup for calmer days.

**Fueling Strategy** — Top off here before heading out. The nearest fuel offshore isn't cheap or convenient.

**Requesting Your Boat** — Call or use the app. Peak times (Friday afternoon, Saturday morning) need more lead time. Weekday mornings? We'll have you on the water in 30 minutes.

**Gulf Stream Days** — Offshore forecast looking clean? The Stream is 15-20 miles straight east from Port Everglades. Once you cross the reef line and hit that blue water, you're in the game.

**Intracoastal Secrets** — Birch State Park has a great sandbar for anchoring. Las Olas Boulevard is restaurant row by water. Head south toward Hollywood for quieter, wider cruising.
